Artwork

Once in a blue moon, a hint of inspiration would land right into my hands and motivate me to create something, anything.

Under the City (2022)

This piece was my submission for the 2022 Victoria Film Festival's Art Van Contest (in partnereship with Modo Car Co-op) where artists from Vancouver Island get to submit a design concept with the theme #YourEyesYourWorld. I made it as one of the five finalists.

Ref.: Jakarta, Indonesia

Cut to the chase, I have an extremely poor eyesight.

People would often ask me what I can see without my glasses. Honestly, not much. But at the same time, a lot.

I remember my mom asked me to describe how I see the world without my glasses. I told her, "I don't see outlines and details but blurred colors and shades. I don't recognized objects from afar but I know when something is there". Most lights tend to become snowflakes in my eyes. Moving around in complete darkness feels like walking towards nothing forever. Do I think these experiences are horrible? Not really.

I grew up in a big city filled with buildings and nightlights everywhere. At times, I would get starstrucked by the pretty view throughout the streets. But there were also moments where I felt overwhelmed and irritated, as if I was drowning in brightness and crowdedness all at once.

So, I thought sketching out the view of my hometown could be a cool idea for this (since it's a part of my world). I had an actual photo of the view as my reference. However, I wanted to draw it as how I would (want to) see it. Hence, I used a charcoal-like brush and random colors to create my work. I wanted it to be a bit blurry/abstract yet bold. Despite the colors

in the photo being dark and gloomy, I intended to make this art work brighter just a bit. I asked a few of my friends and family members what they see. They all answered something along the line of "under the sea" and "lots of corals". However, one person saw this as a "flower field under a blue sky".

Before I received their feedback, my original plan was to add black outlines and sketch out the buildings (from the photo). However, I thought it would defeat the purpose of why I made this art work, which is to see the unseen.

In the end, I was happy with how it turned out. Anyone can see it as whatever they like and they will never be wrong.
